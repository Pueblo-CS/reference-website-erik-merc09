History of American Football

American Football originated in the United States by a man called Walter Camp, who was both a player and coach by making the specific rules that are imbedded into football today. The first ever American Football match to be played was November 6, 1869 between two colleges which were Rutgers and Princeton and took place in in New Brunswick, New Jersey with about 100 people watching which ended in Rutgers winning 6-4. After around 50 years later the NFL association is created on September 17, 1920 in Canton, Ohio with 10 original football teams and 4 joining later on that year. After around 50 years pass the first ever American Football superbowl is played on January 15, 1967 by the Green Bay Packers and the Kansas City Chiefs which was the defeat of the Chiefs 35-10. As of today, there has been 59 total superbowls that have been played and around 32 NFL teams that have been created since the start which has been a around 60 years of football being made.
